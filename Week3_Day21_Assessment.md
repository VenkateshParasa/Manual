## ISTQB Foundation Level Preparation

### Exam Details
- **Questions:** 40 multiple choice
- **Duration:** 60 minutes
- **Passing Score:** 26/40 (65%)
- **Format:** Computer-based or paper-based
- **Cost:** Varies by country ($200-$300 USD typically)
- **Validity:** Lifetime (no expiration)

### Exam Topics & Weightage
1. **Fundamentals of Testing (26%)** - ~10 questions
   - Seven testing principles
   - Test process
   - Psychology of testing

2. **Testing Throughout SDLC (16%)** - ~6 questions
   - SDLC models
   - Test levels
   - Test types

3. **Static Testing (11%)** - ~4 questions
   - Reviews
   - Static analysis

4. **Test Techniques (31%)** - ~12 questions
   - Black-box techniques
   - White-box techniques
   - Experience-based techniques

5. **Test Management (16%)** - ~6 questions
   - Test planning
   - Risk management
   - Defect management

### Study Strategy

**4 Weeks Before Exam:**
- Complete this 100-question assessment
- Review all incorrect answers
- Study ISTQB Glossary (key terms)
- Read ISTQB Syllabus v4.0

**3 Weeks Before:**
- Take 5 practice exams (40 questions each)
- Score consistently 80%+
- Focus on weak areas
- Memorize seven testing principles

**2 Weeks Before:**
- Take 3 more practice exams
- Review test design techniques thoroughly
- Practice calculations (DRE, coverage, etc.)
- Understand SDLC models

**1 Week Before:**
- Take 2 final practice exams
- Review glossary daily
- Rest well before exam
- Arrive early on exam day

### Exam Tips

**During Exam:**
1. Read each question carefully (watch for "NOT", "EXCEPT")
2. Eliminate obviously wrong answers
3. Choose the BEST answer (not just correct)
4. Manage time: 1.5 minutes per question
5. Mark uncertain questions for review
6. Don't leave questions blank (no negative marking)
7. Review marked questions with remaining time

**Common Traps:**
- Questions with "NOT" or "EXCEPT" (read carefully)
- Multiple correct answers (choose BEST one)
- Similar-sounding terms (verification vs validation)
- Calculation questions (practice formulas)

### Remember These Key Principles:

**1. Quality is Everyone's Responsibility**
